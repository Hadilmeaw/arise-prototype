## 1. Purpose of this document

This document explains the ARISE clinical research investigation to candidates considering participation. Reading it carefully, discussing it with relatives or trusted people, and asking any questions to the clinical staff is encouraged before any decision is taken.

Participation in the investigation is entirely voluntary. The decision to take part or to decline does not affect the care received at the clinical site in any way.

## 2. About the ARISE investigation

### 2.1 The problem being studied

The Sit-to-Stand transfer (standing up from a seated position) is one of the most frequent movements of daily life and a basic indicator of mobility, strength, and balance. Difficulty in performing it is associated with an elevated risk of falls.

Today, the assessment of the Sit-to-Stand transfer in the clinic relies on visual observation by the therapist and on simple timed tests. There is no easy way to obtain objective and detailed measurements of how the patient performs the movement during a session.

### 2.2 The investigational device

ARISE is a new tool developed by Innovina S.r.l. that uses a camera to record the Sit-to-Stand transfer and produces an objective description of the movement. During the session, ARISE provides short audio and visual cues to support self-correction. After the session, the therapist reviews a summary of performance and longitudinal progress.

The system does not touch the body. It does not deliver any energy. It uses a single camera placed in front of the chair during the rehabilitation session.

### 2.3 Objectives of the investigation

The investigation has two main objectives.

The first objective is to measure the accuracy of ARISE against a laboratory reference. The ARISE measurements are compared with the measurements produced by a laboratory system that uses small reflective markers placed on the body, which is the established reference for the measurement of human movement.

The second objective is to assess how useful and how acceptable the device is for therapists and for participants during routine rehabilitation sessions.

## 3. Who is invited

Eligible candidates for the investigation are:

- Adults of 18 years of age or older
- Patients undergoing rehabilitation that includes the Sit-to-Stand transfer at Studio Buccarella, or healthy adults volunteering in the reference group
- Persons able to perform the Sit-to-Stand transfer without external physical assistance
- Persons who can read and understand this document and provide informed consent

Candidates are not invited if any of the following applies:

- The Sit-to-Stand transfer is medically contraindicated
- Informed consent cannot be provided and no legal representative is available
- A recent injury or surgery of the legs or trunk would prevent safe participation
- The candidate is currently participating in another clinical investigation
- The Principal Investigator judges that participation would not be safe

The Principal Investigator confirms eligibility according to the criteria above. The full criteria are described in the Clinical Investigation Plan, available on request.

## 4. Participation overview

### 4.1 Visit schedule

Participation is organised in the following visits.

| Visit | Where | Activities |
|---|---|---|
| V0, Screening | Studio Buccarella | Eligibility check, baseline information, signature of the Informed Consent Form |
| V1 to Vn, ARISE sessions | Studio Buccarella | Sit-to-Stand repetitions performed in front of the ARISE camera, with the device active and the biofeedback running. The investigation involves **typically 4 to 8 sessions** distributed over the participation period |
| VR, Reference measurement | REHELAB laboratory, University of Genoa | Sit-to-Stand repetitions measured at the same time by ARISE and by the laboratory reference |
| VF, Follow-up | Studio Buccarella | Short questionnaire about the device, brief exit interview, recording of any event since the previous session |

### 4.2 During each session

Sessions take place in the gym at Studio Buccarella or in a similar room. Sit-to-Stand repetitions are performed from a chair, with brief recovery periods in between. The session is supervised by a qualified rehabilitation professional. The ARISE device records the movement and provides short audio and visual cues to support self-correction.

A request to stop or to take a break can be made at any time. Any sensation of fatigue, dizziness, or discomfort must be communicated to the supervising clinician immediately.

### 4.3 Duration

Each session lasts approximately **30 to 45 minutes**. Total participation typically extends over **2 to 4 weeks**, including all visits described above.

## 5. Possible benefits

Participation does not guarantee a direct benefit. The additional structured Sit-to-Stand training may provide an indirect benefit to participants in the patient cohort. The main expected benefit is to society, through the evidence collected to improve rehabilitation tools for people in similar conditions.

## 6. Possible risks and inconveniences

The device does not contact the participant and does not deliver any energy. The main risks during participation are the ones associated with performing the Sit-to-Stand transfer itself — the same movement already performed during routine rehabilitation. A supervising clinician is present during every session.

The possible inconveniences include:

| Inconvenience | Measure to reduce it |
|---|---|
| Tiredness from repeated Sit-to-Stand repetitions | Structured recovery periods between repetitions, with the option to stop at any time |
| Mild muscular discomfort | Session supervision by a qualified rehabilitation professional, with the option to stop at any time |
| Mild confusion or distraction from the biofeedback | Familiarisation phase before the first measurement session, with the option to deactivate the biofeedback |
| Time required for the additional visits | Visits are scheduled to minimise the impact on daily life. The reference measurement at REHELAB is the only visit at a different site |
| Travel to the REHELAB laboratory for the reference measurement session | [TO BE COMPLETED on the reimbursement policy, if any] |

In the event of a fall or injury during a session, the supervising clinician provides immediate assistance. The investigation is covered by an insurance policy described in Section 10.

## 7. If something goes wrong

Any concern about safety, or any sensation that something is not right during participation, should be discussed immediately with the supervising clinician, the Principal Investigator, or Innovina. The contact details are listed in Section 10.

Any harm caused by participation in the investigation may give rise to compensation under the insurance policy that Innovina has in place, also described in Section 10.

## 8. Right to withdraw

Participation is voluntary. Withdrawal from the investigation is possible at any time, without any obligation to provide a justification, and without any consequence on the care received at the clinical site.

On withdrawal, the participant may request that the personal data collected up to that moment be deleted, except for any data that the law requires to be kept and except for any data that has already been combined with the data of other participants in a way that no longer allows individual identification.

The decision to withdraw is recorded in the source documents. The supervising clinician remains available to discuss the decision on request.

## 9. Processing of personal data

This section explains how personal data is collected, used, and protected. The legal framework is the **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), Regulation (EU) 2016/679**, and the Italian legislation that implements it. A detailed assessment of the data-protection risks and of the measures applied is provided in the **Data Protection Impact Assessment**, available on request.

### 9.1 Data that is collected

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Identification data | Name, date of birth, contact information. Held by the clinical site only and replaced by a participant code for the rest of the investigation |
| Clinical data | Medical history relevant to the investigation, baseline assessment, clinical scores collected during the sessions |
| Video recordings | Video of the Sit-to-Stand transfer during the sessions |
| Kinematic data | Numbers produced by ARISE from the video, including joint angles, velocities, and detected events |
| Questionnaire responses | Responses to the usability and acceptability questionnaires |
| Device telemetry | Technical events of the device, without identifying information |

### 9.2 Reasons for the processing

The processing rests on the explicit consent of the participant for use in the investigation and for the training of the AI components of ARISE. Adverse-event recording is also carried out to comply with the legal obligations applicable to clinical investigations of medical devices.

### 9.3 Who has access to the data

| Party | Role |
|---|---|
| Studio Buccarella | The clinical site. Its clinical staff who supervise the sessions have access to identification data and clinical data |
| Innovina S.r.l. | The manufacturer of ARISE. Innovina has access to the data with the participant's name replaced by a code |
| DINOGMI, University of Genoa | The scientific partner. DINOGMI has access to the coded data for scientific analysis and for the reference measurement at REHELAB |
| Ethics Committee | Access to source documents for the purpose of an audit only |
| National regulatory authorities | Access only for the purpose of an inspection |

Innovina S.r.l. and the University of Genoa are jointly responsible for the use of the data, in accordance with the agreement between them. Studio Buccarella handles the data on their behalf.

### 9.4 Where the data is stored

The data is stored on infrastructure located in the European Union. It is encrypted at rest and in transit. Access is restricted to authorised personnel and logged. The data is not transferred outside the European Economic Area.

### 9.5 How long the data is kept

| Category | Retention period |
|---|---|
| Identification data | For the duration of the investigation and for the minimum period required by law |
| Clinical data and case report forms | At least 10 years after the end of the investigation, as required by the Medical Devices Regulation |
| Video recordings | [PENDING — see DPIA. Indicative period: 5 years for the corpus used to train the AI components, with the explicit option to opt out of the long-term use] |
| Anonymised aggregate data used in publications | Indefinitely, since the data is no longer personal data |

### 9.6 Participant rights

The participant has the following rights with respect to personal data: access, correction of inaccurate data, deletion (within the limits described in Section 8), restriction of processing, data portability, objection on grounds relating to the particular situation, and the right to lodge a complaint with the Italian Data Protection Authority.

These rights can be exercised by contacting the Data Protection Officer of Innovina S.r.l. at the address listed in Section 10.

### 9.7 Automated decision making

Personal data is not subject to automated decision making that produces legal effects or that significantly affects the participant.

The ARISE algorithm produces real-time feedback during the session to support self-correction. The feedback does not constitute a clinical diagnosis. Decisions on the rehabilitation plan are made by the qualified rehabilitation professional and not by the device.

### 9.8 Use of data for future research

The pseudonymised data may be used for future scientific research in the field of motor rehabilitation. Any future research will be subject to the prior approval of an Ethics Committee and will be conducted under the same safeguards as the present investigation. The Informed Consent Form provides a separate consent box for this purpose, which is independent from participation in the present investigation.
